mRNA Degradation and Translation Repression, 
in P-Bodies
n Processing bodies (P-bodies or PBs) are discrete 
collections of RNAs and proteins that are involved in 
mRNA decay and translational repression.
n P-bodies are cellular foci where mRNAs are destroyed or 
translationally repressed
n These cellular foci are enriched in enzymes that 
deadenylate mRNAs, decap mRNAs and catalyze 5’ to 3’ 
degradation of mRNAs
n Degradation of mRNA occurs by a non-RNAi-like 
mechanism 


--- Page 62 ---
Relief of Repression in P-Bodies
n Although many mRNAs are degraded in P-bodies, many 
others are held and repressed there
n In a liver cell line (Huh7), translation of the CAT-1 mRNA is 
repressed by the miRNA miR-122 and the mRNA is 
sequestered in P-bodies
n Upon starvation, the translation repression of the CAT-1 
mRNA is relieved and the miRNA migrates from P-bodies 
to polysomes
n This derepression and translocation of the mRNA depends 
on the mRNA-binding protein HuR and on its binding site 
in the 3’-UTR of the mRNA
