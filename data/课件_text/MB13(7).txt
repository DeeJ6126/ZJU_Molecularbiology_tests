Genetic Codons
Genetic code:  the correspondence between the 
sequence of the four bases in nuclei acids and the 
sequence of the 20 amino acids in protein.
 The set of three-base code words (codons) in 
mRNAs that stand for the 20 amino acids in proteins. 


--- Page 46 ---
• Triplet : A three base sequence (codon) specifies an aa.
• Commaless: There are no untranslated gaps in the code. 
• Unambiguous: A particular codon specifies only one aa
• Degenerate: More than one triplet codes for a given 
amino acid.
• Start codon: AUG=Met
• Stop codon: UAA, UAG, UGA

Wobble hypothesis (摆动假说) 
•
In 1966， Frances Crick  hypothesized  that the first two 
bases of a codon must pair correctly with the anticodon  
according to Watson-Crick base-pairing rules, but the last 
base of codon can wobble from its  normal position to form 
unusual base pair with the anticodon.
•
Wobble base pair: The ability of  the third base of a 
codon to shift slightly to form a non-Watson-Crick base 
pair with the first base of an anticodon, thus allowing a 
tRNA to translate more than one codon.

Translational control
•  Prokaryotic translational control: in initiation stage
   Prokaryotic mRNAs are very short-lived, so control of 
translation is not common in these organisms. However, 
some translational control does occur.
• mRNA secondary structure can govern translation 
initiation (σ32  accumulating in less than a minute 
after heat shock.)
• Controlled by feed-back repression


--- Page 57 ---
•
Under normal temperature 37ºC, there is the base pairing between the 
initiation codon (boxed) and a region near the 3’-end of the mRNA, 
forming “stem I, which prevent the translation of σ32 mRNA.
•
When heat shock (42 ºC), σ32 begins accumulating in less than a minute, 
which is too little time for regulation on transcriptional level. 
•
When the temperature rises, the base pairs causing this secondary 
structure melt, unmasking the initiation codon.
