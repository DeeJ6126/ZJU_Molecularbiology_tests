The Meselson-Stahl Experiment
Different replication ways can produce different DNA progenies.  
Therefore, if the progeny DNA could be separated from parental 
DNA, the replication model can be determined.  Based on this 
idea, Matthew Meselson and Franklin Stahl used 15N isotope to 
create replicated DNA molecules with different weights, then  
ultracentrifuged to separate the DNA progenies 
不同的复制方式产生不同的子代DNA分子。因此，如能把新合成的DNA分
子从模板DNA分子分离开，则复制模式就能确定。根据这种思想， 
Matthew Meselson and Franklin Stahl设计用15N同位素产生不同重量
的DNA分子及超速离心机分离不同重量DNA分子的实验。

DNA polymerase & Okazaki’s model 
•
The discovery of DNA polymerase by Arthur Kornberg has 
revealed that DNA polymerase synthesize DNA in one direction 
only: 5’ to 3’.
(Arthur Kornberg发现了DNA聚合酶并证明了该酶严格按模板3’-5’方向
合成DNA, 1959 Nobel Prize).
•
Okazaki proposed “Semi-discontinuous model”
•
Okazaki fragment : DNA replication is Semi-discontinuous. One 
strand is replicated continuously in the direction of the 
movement of the replicating fork; the other is replicated 
discontinuously as 1-2 kb Okazaki fragments in the opposite 
direction. This allows both strands to be replicated in the 5’ to 3’.

Features of DNA replication
（DNA复制的特征)
1. Semi-conservative (半保留复制)
2. Semi-discontinuous (半不连续复制)
3. DNA polymerases are template- and primer-dependent 
   (DNA 聚合酶依赖于DNA模板与引物)
4. All DNA polymerases synthesize DNA in the 5‘ to 3’ direction, 
reading the template 3‘ to 5’ 
(所有的DNA聚合酶按5‘-3’方向[模板DNA为3‘-5’方向]合成DNA)
5.  DNA polymerases require the four deoxynucleoside 
triphosphates (dATP, dGTP, dCTP, and dTTP)
 (DNA聚合酶合成DNA需要4种脱氧核糖核酸分子: dATP, dGTP, dCTP, 
and dTTP)

Topoisomerase (拓扑异构酶) 
•
Unwinding a helix for replication produces 
topological strain and supercoiling. This strain 
must be relieved or replication cannot continue. 
This is the function of enzymes called 
topoisomerase.  
•
Two types of topoisomerase:
1.
Introduce temporary single-strand breaks (nicks) 
called Type I topoisomerases 
2.
Introduce temporary double-strand breaks, 
called Type II topoisomerases.

Type I topoisomerase
•
These are also called nicking-closing enzymes and function to 
relax supercoiled circular molecules. These enzymes function

Telomerases
•
Problem: when a newly synthesized lagging strand has been copied 
near the 3' end of one strand. Now you need to have a primer to 
start another lagging strand. But how do you do this beyond the 3' 
end of the chromosome strand? 
•
Telomerases: The enzyme is related to reverse transcriptase, in 
that it has a small RNA template as a part of the enzyme structure. 
•
Telomeres have repeated sequences called telomere repeats. The 
RNA template of telomerase is used to add a telomere repeat to the 
3 ' end. An RNA primer is then synthesized as a copy of this 
extension to the DNA and the last lagging strand is made. Later the 
extension is taken off along with the RNA primer.

Ways of DNA replication
•
Bidirectional replication (双向复制):  Two replicating 
forks moving in opposite directions away from the origin. 
•
Unidirectional replication(单向复制):  with one 
replicating fork move from the other
•
Rolling circle replication(滚环复制): one strand of a 
double-stranded circular DNA remains intact & serves as the 
template for elongation of the other strand at a nick.
